ABOUT US

We are Welcome.us, a

non-profit dedicated to celebrating a United States that is fueled by an immigrant tradition.

 

In the United States, with a good idea and enough hard work, anything is possible. The entrepreneurial drive and spirit of our country is built on our diversity of origins. It is what drew the first people to the US and what continues to drive American business. American success is a result of our many distinct experiences, not in spite of it.

We're kicking things off this June with the first ever Immigrant Heritage Month, a nationwide effort to gather and share inspirational stories of immigration in America.

 

 

Immigrants have played a starring role in the history and culture of the United States. For the month of June, we will focus on telling some of the stories that shaped this country. The month will include partnerships with corporations, media outlets, organizations, celebrities, athletes, faith, civic

and political leaders.

PEOPLE

Rob Matei

My family is Romanian and I was born there. Ultimately, my parents wanted me to come to the U.S. because people who are at the top of their field, whatever that field might be, end up in the U.S. I went to Stanford and I was around so many people who were trying to do tech and trying to do start ups – it was really inspiring.

Mariana Castro

I came here when I was four years old from Brazil. My mom had a work sponsor, and I got to get my papers through her. I went to high school and college here and it's been a great opportunity in comparison to cousins and friends who stayed in Brazil. Being Brazilian has helped me at my job at Salesforce because I work with the Brazilian market and I understand the cultural aspect of that community.

Diego Garcia

I came to the United States when I was 11 years old in the year 2000. It was a very tumultuous time in Colombia and my parents wanted to provide us with a stable living place. I was able to go to the University of Florida and studied nuclear engineering and political science. It's my American dream to be able to talk about issues of energy and to engineer. I love energy policy, it's what drives me.
